Quick Tip:

This guide has 3 skills. The first two skills are the must have to live in America. The last skill is the must have to pursue your American Dream. This guide works best if you are still in school. This is because the school setting is really set up to help you. Keep in mind that whether you are a teenage immigrant or a middle-aged immigrant, it is never too late to go back to school.

Skill 1

Overcome The Language Barrier

-To succeed in America, you need to know how to speak some English.

Stage 1: Learn words one by one

An English sentence is made of words, so start with words.

How Yuteng did it?

Yuteng started out by using index cards to memorize words, writing words in the front and definitions in the back. He memorized each word's pronunciation first, then its meaning and spelling. To memorize pronunciation, he listened to a digital dictionary that can say the word. Then he followed by saying the word out loud multiple times. Knowing how to say the word was the most important thing to him, because his goal was to talk to someone clearly. Once he made some index cards, he reviewed them on a daily basis until their pronunciations and definitions stayed in his head.

Our Comment:

To memorize new words is very slow, but once you learn them already, to review and go through them is super fast. Let's say you start out by making 10 index cards for 10 words. It might take you 20 minutes to memorize these 10 new words, but it would only take you less than 1 minute to review all of them. This is how our brain works and keep this brain trick in mind. When you memorize some new words today, you should constantly review them on a daily basis for about a month to get them really into your head. If you don't review the new words, you will forget them very quickly. Memorizing new words is not a day thing; it is a month thing. Also, the advantage of using index card is that it is very portable. You can take the index cards with you and review them when you have time, such as in class, on a train, or before bed. Again, you only need 1 minute or less to review 10 words.

Stage 2: Talk to people

Talking to people is the best way to overcome the language barrier. After you have learned some words, it is time to put them to use by talking to people.

How Jay did it?

Jay came to the U.S. from Korea when he was 14. When he first came, he was very bad at speaking. Luckily, he lived with his cousins who are native speakers and also are around his ages. They talked to each other a lot and his cousins corrected him a lot. It has been 4 years since then, and now he can speak as good as a native speaker, if not better.

Our Comment:

We all start out to be poor English speakers. Getting good at it is a slow process that takes a lot of practices and time to improve. Even living in a good learning environment, Jay said it took him 3 years to perfect his speaking skills. There is simply no shortcut. The more you talk, the better you will be able to verbalize your thoughts and the more comfortable you will feel when talking with people in English. So if you want to improve your speaking skills, you have to talk a lot with other people, like your classmates, your teachers, your coworkers, your friends, some strangers, or yourself. You can learn to say many everyday sentences in a very good website - TalkEnglish.com and practice saying the sentences multiple times to get comfortable saying them. The website also provides some very good phrases for different scenarios. For example, in a scenario when you don't get to hear what the other person is saying, which happens all the time, it suggests you to say "What was that again?" or "Pardon me?". Lastly, there are also many other ways where you can expose yourself to English, like watching movies and TV, or reading books. These are good ways, but talking to people is the best way to overcome the language barrier. The key is talk, talk more and talk a lot more.

Stage Beyond: Talking in English as if talking in mother tongue

By the end of completing Stage 2, you will have built some solid foundations in English and can talk to people easily. You can say goodbye to index cards, as you can learn more and more by your everyday interactions with others. You will pick up some jokes in English and joke around with people. You will pick up what is going on in your community, your city, the U.S. and the world. If you are studying in school, you can learn things more in depth from classes, textbooks, your friends and your teachers.

Skill 2

Reach Out To Others If Stuck

-On your way to pursue your American Dream, you would face situations when you don't know what to do. A smart solution is to reach out to other people and ask for their helps.

Stage 1: You are nervous when reaching out to others

How John did it?

In freshman year of high school, the U.S. history class was John's nightmare. He didn't know anything about U.S. history and he failed many tests and quizzes. His U.S. history teacher Mr. Williams looked big and scary, but he decided to talk to him and ask for his help. One day after class, he tolded Mr. Williams that he found this class very hard and asked him how could he improve his performance. Mr. Williams was very friendly and told John that he can always come to his office in lunch hour if he had any questions. Mr. Williams even found a senior student to tutor John individually. Thanks to this. John started to catch up slowly. (Even though John performance was near the edge of failing, he ended up getting a 90 in his class. There must be something special about people like Mr. Williams. They can somehow see that their students were trying and saw them beyond their failing test performance.)

Our Comment:

It is normal to be nervous when we first start to reach out to others for helps, because we don't know what they are going to do. But it turns out that our concerns are incorrect most of the time. In John's case, Mr. Williams is not intimidating as he thought, but is actually very friendly and willing to help. So don't afraid to ask for helps, especially in a school setting, where 99% of the people in school are willing to offer their helps. It is important to take action and reach out to other people, so they can get to understand your needs and help you with whatever they know. Of course, there are also people out there who are not going to help you, but someone eventually will. Always be respectful to people and be grateful to those who offer their hands and help you. Now, see if you can think of something that troubles you and reach out to someone who can help you.

Stage 2: You are comfortable reaching out to others in many scenarios

Once you taste the benefits of reaching out to people, you would start to reach out more often and in more scenarios.

How Xitang did it?

Xitang was studying in New York University that has the reputation of costing students a lot of money to attend. Very true! He has to take out loans to pay his tuition bill. So he decided to go to the financial aid office to see if it is possible to get some scholarship. He explained his situation to a financial aid counselor Andrew. Andrew was a very nice people, told him that there is a form which he can fill out to possibly get some additional scholoarships. Andrew even showed him how to fill out the form step by step. After filling out the form with some good things he've been doing in the school, NYU financial aid office actually awarded Xitang with some extra scholarships. In addition, Xitang happened also to tell his financial situation in college to his high school college office counselor Ms. Rosados when he visited his high school before Christmas . She warmly told him "Come to the college office after the break. We will help you find some scholarships to apply".

Our Comment:

Our world is the product of human interactions. It is very hard to do everything on your own without the helps from other people. Reaching out to others allows you to see others' view points and get their expertises to help you. There are tons of ways to reach out. If you are stuck with some questions in mind, you know you can google it and reach out to the online community. If you have troubles with your personal life, you know you can reach out to your school counselor, your family members, or people who know you well. And if you have troubles on your ways to pursue your American Dream, you know you can reach out to Xitang and Yuteng and our advisory borad right here. It is a people world. You are never alone. Actively reach out to others and be surprised of what you are about to find out.

Stage Beyond: People reach out to you

By the end of completing Stage 2, you should have reached out to many people and you actually know enough so that other people start to reach out to you for helps. You will start to become more and more appreciative in life, as you begin to see how it would have been impossible for you to get to whom you are today without the helps and supports from others. You will begin to help others like those who have helped you. We honestly would not be making this website and getting to whom we are today without the helps and supports from our family, friends, teachers, and many others. They are the reasons that we are here and reaching out to you.

Once you've mastered Skills 1 and 2, you should be very proud of yourself, because you can easily survive in this country now, as you can speak its language and know to look for helps when needed. We congratulate your success and if you are interested in knowing how to reach your American Dream, please go on and read Skill 3.

Skill 3

Do It Yourself

Other people would help you a lot on your way to pursue your American Dream, but you would soon find out that there are things you have to do it on your own.

Stage 1: Start with something you wanted

Weichao wanted to become good at playing basketball.

How Weichao did it?

Weichao first came into contact with basketball in his third grade gym class. He was a rookie and played bad at it, but he liked it a lot and wanted to be good at it. He decided to go to the court and practice his jump shot for two hours every Sunday. He began his training by shooting basketball along the 10 different positions of the free throw lane. He first could get 4 out of 10 balls into the basket, then 8 out of 10 with weeks of practice and finally 10 out of 10 with months of practice. After this, he started to practice the more challenging free throws and three point shots. With some good shooting skills, he began playing matches in teams, but he kept losing and failed to make it into the school team. He soon found out that this is because basketball was also about teamwork and disciplines other than shootings. He started to sit next to the court everyday after school and watch how people play for the first two weeks, and began to watch how the professionals play in CBA and NBA later on. With weeks after weeks of learning and practicing, he made it to the school basketball team and even became the captain of the team. What he have learned from basketball in China continue to carry on with him when he moved to America. Basketball has trained him to be flexible, athletic and cooperative, and he was placed first in the NYC PSAL Boys Badminton Double Championships in 2014 Spring with his partner Lekka.

Our Comment:

If you want something, get moving. You don't need to have any fancy plan but action. Once you start moving, you will find your next step. In Weichao's case, even though he was bad at playing basketball when started, he came up with a training plan the moment he got to the court. Then interest and desire play the second most important roles, because your interest and desire decide whether you will go on or give up. Because of Weichao's strong interest and desire to excel in basketball, he was able to maintain his rigor training every Sunday and continue to watch the NBA even till today (Basketball becomes part of his life). In addition, a great thing about interest is that interest will take you to places you never imagined you would be in. Following his interest, Weichao might know that he would become increasingly better at playing basketball, but he would not imagine that he will become the captain of his school team and even go on and win the badminton championship in NYC. So, if you have something you want, just go after it right now. All the plans and thoughts will come to you once you start moving. If you really like what you are doing, you will have endless energy and motivation to keep going on. Eventually, your interest will bring you to places you never thought possible. (On the other hand, if you give up on something easily, it might just be the fact that you don't want it and interest in it that much. If this happens, you should simply move on and find other things that you really want.)

Stage 2: Go after more things you wanted

Xitang wanted to be strong.

How Xitang did it?

In his sophomore year of high school, Xitang was very weak and he really wanted to be strong. He didn't have money to go to the gym, but thanks to many free workout equipments(like bars) provided in many parks, he was able to make the first move and started training himself in the park. When he first started, he could barely do 3 pull-ups. But he continued to go to the park everyday after school. He started to do 5 pull-ups with weeks of training, then 8 pull-ups with months of training, and then 20s with years of training. Now, he can even do bar flips and muscle ups easily, and he is working on something more interesting and challenging, like human flag and planche.

Xitang wanted to get a good SAT score.

How Xitang did it?

In his sophomore year of high school, Xitang began to think about getting himself into a good college. For this to be possible, he needed to have a good SAT score. At that time, his performance was about 1400s and he was aiming to get 1800, because 1800 was good enough to get into most good schools. He bought a copy of College Board's Official SAT Study Guide, started reading it page by page, and really tried to understand the contents. There were also 10 practice test in the back of the book and he began to do the practice test one by one. His performance was gradually increased from 1400 to 1600 to 1800 within a year. In the meanwhile, he also did some researches on the internet and found out that the College Board actually released the real SAT tests from January, May and October to the students. There are more than 30 previous SAT tests he found online and he again began doing them one by one using his free time over the summer. His performance was increased from 1800 to 2000 to about 2200 within two years.

Our Comment:

Going after things you wanted is the best way to train and understand yourself. When you first started out to pursue something, you probably would look down on yourself, underestimate yourself and doubt yourself. It is perfectly normal and people all start out doing things with little confidence. In Xitang's case, he thought he was weak and stupid in 10th grade, and he really admired people who can do about 10 pull ups and people who got 1800s in SAT. However, as you continue to go after the things you wanted, you will start to see your growth and recognize your own capability. You will start to realize that your body and brain learn a lot faster than you think. After a lot of practices, your confidence starts to kick in as you get better doing something and know more about something. In Xitang's case, he started to catch up on people he once admired with a lot of practices and even surpass them with a lot a lot more practices. What our experience saying is that you are capable of achieving anything you wanted. As long as you really want something, you will keep working on it and getting closer to it. You should see no difference between you and the celebrities, the presidents, the olympians or us... except they had put a lot of efforts and practices before getting what they wanted. At the end, it all just comes down to how badly you want something. If you really want it, you will continue working on it years after years. The accumulation of small steps you made everyday for many years will eventually bring your American Dream come true...... Now, go after something you wanted and work on it everyday, you will learn a lot about yourself and find a lot of interesting things we didn't cover along your way. (Sometimes, you might not know what you want. This happens a lot, as everyone will lose a sense of direction at some point in their life. A good way to go through this is to read other's autobiography. Reading someone else's autobiography is like living his/her life once. And you can quickly get inspired by their visions and experience, and begin seeing a new direction in your life. )

Stage Beyond: Help others go after what they want

By the end of completing stage 2, you should have gotten a lot of things you wanted and are very confident about what you can achieve. Reaching your American Dreams is just a matter of time and more efforts. Happiness comes if you reach what you wanted, but you will start to see that more happiness comes if you help others reach what they wanted.

(Today is the most enlightening time of human history. More and more people join the causes of helping to create a better world, helping others to unlock their potentials and helping others to reach what they wanted. In a big scale, Facebook founder Mark and his wife Priscilla vow to donate 99% of their facebook shares for education, science, health and community building to benefit the next generation. In a small scale, ShawnTina, a counselor in NYU and a board member of us, starts a non for profit to help and empower youth in NYC. And you have Yuteng and Xitang right here, creating this website hoping to help fellow immigrants in some ways.)

Congratulations on finishing reading this guide and best wishes to your pursuit of your American Dream! We, too, are pursuing our American Dreams. Yuteng wants to work for his dream company Google and Xitang wants to become a leading figure in high tech, living in the frontier of possibility and helping to bring a wave of technology revolution in China and across the world...
